PAGES 1–2: CORE THESIS

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

ServiceNow is the dominant enterprise workflow orchestration platform at $12.88B revenue (FY2025, EDGAR confirmed), with $28.2B in total RPO (+22.5% YoY) and Now Assist crossing $600M in ACV — nearly doubled YoY in Q4 2025, the first enterprise SaaS AI product to demonstrate incremental contract value at scale. The stock has been cut in half from its 52-week high of $211 on macro and multiple-compression fears — yet FY2026 subscription guidance of $15.53–15.57B (+20%) remains intact. At ~20x non-GAAP forward earnings, the market is now pricing a structurally superior compounder as if the AI monetisation story has failed. It hasn't. This is the most compelling entry point in years. Size at 5–7%.

THE BULL CASE

  • Now Assist crossed $600M ACV — the AI monetisation proof point is no longer speculative. NNACV nearly doubled YoY in Q4 2025. 244 deals >$1M NNACV, including 7 deals >$10M. Monthly active users grew 25%. Customers renewing and adding more assist packs when they run out of tokens — a consumption signal, not a licence renewal.
  • $28.2B RPO (+22.5%) at 2.2× annual revenue is fortress-level forward visibility. No other enterprise SaaS company at $13B+ revenue is growing total backlog at 22%. cRPO of $12.85B (+21%) locks in the next 12 months of revenue with near certainty.
  • Moveworks closes the "AI front door" gap — and McDermott's framing is now sharper. Q4 transcript: "AI doesn't replace enterprise orchestration. It depends on it." This is the non-consensus truth. Every AI agent deployment creates workflow complexity that requires a control plane — governance, audit trails, multi-system routing, compliance.
  • Valuation re-rate: the bear case has been largely priced in. Stock down 35% YTD, now trading at ~20x non-GAAP forward earnings — a level last seen before Now Assist was a product. The original memo's "55–60x non-GAAP forward P/E" was a labelling error.
  • $5B buyback + $2B ASR is accretive at current price, not just dilution management. At $99, the $2B ASR repurchases ~20M shares (~2% of float) — twice the impact of the same programme at $200.

THE BEAR CASE (Munger Inversion)

  • Primary inversion: Microsoft wins the enterprise AI orchestration layer before ServiceNow cements it. Microsoft has Teams in virtually every ServiceNow customer. If Copilot handles 60% of tier-1 IT tickets embedded in the interface employees already live in, CIOs may ask: why are we paying the Now Assist premium?
  • Negative Lollapalooza test (Munger): Three forces acting simultaneously: Microsoft Copilot commoditises the AI front-end, Enterprise IT budget freeze in macro slowdown, SBC dilution drag (~$1.93B/year, 15% of revenue) suppresses FCF per share.
  • SBC at 15% of revenue remains the structural drag. GAAP-to-non-GAAP spread is ~17 percentage points. The $2B ASR roughly offsets one year of SBC — net dilution compression is modest.
  • Moveworks integration risk. AI-native employee experience is fiercely contested (Microsoft, Glean, Google). If Moveworks doesn't integrate cleanly within 18 months, the acquisition becomes an expensive talent acquisition.
  • Growth deceleration arithmetic. At 20% growth, 20x non-GAAP forward P/E is defensible. At 16–17% (one stumble quarter), re-rate risk to 15–17x non-GAAP is possible — a 15–20% additional drawdown from here.

LETTER TO MANAGEMENT

To Bill McDermott and Gina Mastantuono:

The Q4 2025 transcript gave us the clearest articulation of ServiceNow's strategic position we have heard: "AI doesn't replace enterprise orchestration. It depends on it." That is a board-level insight stated with conviction — and the market has not yet priced it. The stock is down 35% from its peak while Now Assist ACV doubled, cRPO grew 21%, and FY2026 guidance came in at +20%. The market is wrong, and you know it — which is why you deployed $2B in accelerated share repurchases.

The most important decision ServiceNow must make in the next 18 months is not whether to accelerate Now Assist commercial adoption — that is working. It is whether to make a definitive architectural commitment to being the enterprise operating system for the agentic AI era, rather than the best workflow platform that AI agents call via API. The Moveworks acquisition and RaptorDB investment signal genuine technical commitment. But the product architecture remains federated: Now Assist as a module, Moveworks as an acquisition to integrate, RaptorDB as infrastructure. A federated approach is defensible against most challengers. It is not defensible against Microsoft if Microsoft ships a unified agent orchestration layer embedded in the interface that 400M enterprise users already live in.

The window to establish the agentic operating system position before Microsoft closes it is approximately 12–18 months. The $5B buyback is the right signal for a mature compounder. For a company in a platform transition this significant, the better deployment of the next tranche of capital is accelerating the technical unification of Moveworks + Now Assist + Workflow Data Fabric + RaptorDB into a single coherent agentic experience — one that no enterprise IT team can walk away from without surrendering 5+ years of workflow intelligence. The share price will follow the platform. Build the platform now.
